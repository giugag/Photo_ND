About the Photovoice initiative

This Photovoice project explores how individuals with neurodevelopmental conditions experience light sensitivity in their daily lives.

Light plays an important role in regulating sleep, circadian rhythms, mood, alertness, and overall wellbeing. Many neurodivergent individuals report heightened sensitivity to light, yet these lived experiences are rarely documented.

Through photography and narrative, this project invites neurodivergent individuals to share how light affects their lives -during the day and at night.

Participants will document their experiences of light in their environments and reflect on questions such as:

  • Light that helps me feel calm
  • Light that disturbs my sleep
  • Daylight vs artificial light
  • My favourite lighting environment
  • Lighting that makes public spaces difficult
  • A place where light affects my sensory experience

The project aims to highlight lived experiences and raise awareness about the role of light in neurodiversity and wellbeing, by creating a visual archive (Gallery) that can inform research and public understanding.

This Photovoice Initiative is part of Photo_ND and is supported by a DkIT–Maynooth University Research Collaboration Seed Fund.

Eligibility

This photovoice project is open to people who identify as neurodivergent (e.g., autistic, ADHD, dyslexic, etc.), whether self-identified or formally diagnosed.

We kindly ask that only people who identify as neurodivergent take part, so that the project reflects these lived experiences.

Guidelines for taking photos

Do’s

  • Capture meaningful moments or environments
  • Respect privacy
  • Focus on light and atmosphere

Don’ts

  • Avoid photographing identifiable people without consent; We strongly recommend that not take photos of people in order to protect their own privacy
  • Avoid photographing children
  • Avoid unsafe situations

Award

A panel of judges will select one winning entry in each of the following three categories:

  • My Light, My Night – Research Choice Award
  • My Light, My Night – Visual Story Award
  • My Light, My Night – Lived Experience Award
Each category winner will receive a €150 voucher. In addition, two special mentions will be awarded, each receiving a €50 voucher.
